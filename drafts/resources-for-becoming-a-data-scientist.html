It's been almost a year since I landed my first data science job.

My Background

In 2012, I began my PhD in computational neuroscience at the University of Chicago. I loved grad school, and I have no regrets. However, as I matured and began focusing on my dissertation, I came to a critical realization: My passion for biology and wet lab work was waining. What I loved most about academic science was really data analysis, programming and statistics (data science).

What was also apparent was that there were tremendous oportunities for ex-academics in data science.

In 2016 I took a break from grad school to do an internship at Palantir. This was a valuable learning experience, and I highly recomend internships for those thinking about data science careers.

I graduated in 2017, moved to the Bay Area and joined the Insight Data Science program, a free felowship for academics transitioning to data science. Insight was incredibly intense, but 3 months after I started the program I landed a job a Cape Analytics, where I work today.
